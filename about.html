About Me

Hello! My name is Jadon Kam and I’m currently a student at Wilfrid Laurier University studying User Experience Design. 

If it wasn’t clear already from my portfolio looking like an art museum, I love art and design! At a young age, I developed an interest in drawing, and as I grew older, I became interested in painting, going to art museums, sculpture and 3D art, digital art, interior design, and more! Another hobby I loved starting at a young age was reading mystery books. I loved learning about each character and trying to find the solution to the mystery before the detective revealed the answer.

As a result of my love for design and my interest in solving problems, I decided to study and become a UX designer. Although the problems I work on as a UX designer aren’t as mysterious as the problems my favourite detectives worked on, I love working on UX problems and couldn’t think of another job I would enjoy more! I look forward to working with others on projects where I can learn what users desire in a product and design a better experience for them. If you are interested in hiring or collaborating with me, please fill out my contact form. Thanks!

What is my favourite project?

My favourite project I’ve done is Travel Kitt! I loved solving the problem of making travel information interesting and engaging for children. As someone who has enjoyed working as a children summer camp counsellor for the past few years, I really enjoyed creating an app for children and having them as the user group we focused on in this project. I also loved what my teammate and I were able to create visually as well as the concept behind the interface to have a scrapbook which families can keep to learn about places and remember their happy experiences on vacation. You can view this project by clicking here!


What am I looking forward to learn more about?

I’m looking forward to continue learning about accessible and inclusive design. Whether that’s designing interfaces or creating products, I’m interested and believe it’s my responsibility as a UX designer to make sure users who need accessible features for sight, hearing, movement, etc. are included in my user-centred design process.
